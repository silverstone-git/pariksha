
### **1. GATE Physics (Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering)**
This exam tests both speed and accuracy. The inclusion of **MSQs** and **NATs** is a huge advantage because they have **no negative marking**.

* **Duration:** 3 Hours (180 mins)
* **Total Marks:** 100
* **Total Questions:** 65
* **Sections:**
    1.  **General Aptitude (GA):** 10 questions (15 marks). Covers verbal and numerical ability.
    2.  **Physics (Core):** 55 questions (85 marks).
* **Question Types & Marking:**
    * **MCQs (Multiple Choice):** 1 or 2 marks. Penalty: **-1/3** or **-2/3** respectively.
    * **MSQs (Multiple Select):** 1 or 2 marks. One or more correct options. **No negative marking.**
    * **NATs (Numerical Answer Type):** 1 or 2 marks. Enter a decimal/integer. **No negative marking.**

### **2. CSIR NET Physical Sciences**
This is a test of "choice." You are given 75 questions but only need to answer 55. If you answer more than the limit, only the *first* 15/20/20 questions are graded.

* **Duration:** 3 Hours (180 mins)
* **Total Marks:** 200
* **Sections & Choice:**
| Section | Total Qs | Max to Attempt | Marks per Correct Q | Negative Marking |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Part A** (Aptitude) | 20 | 15 | +2 | -0.5 |
| **Part B** (Core Physics) | 25 | 20 | +3.5 | -0.875 |
| **Part C** (Advanced) | 30 | 20 | +5 | -1.25 |

---

### **3. TIFR GS (Physics)**
TIFR is widely considered the most conceptually difficult. It is not about how many questions you solve, but the depth of your understanding.

* **Duration:** 3 Hours (180 mins)
* **Total Marks:** 100
* **Total Questions:** 40
* **Sections:**
    * **Section A (Basic):** 25 questions. Tests fundamental undergraduate physics.
        * **Marking:** +3 for correct, **-1 for incorrect**.
    * **Section B (Advanced):** 15 questions. Tests graduate-level depth and advanced problem-solving.
        * **Marking:** +5 for correct, **0 for incorrect (No Negative Marking)**.
* **Strategy Tip:** Since Section B has no negative marking, you should always attempt all 15 questions there, even if you are only 50% sure.

---

### **4. BARC OCES (Scientific Officer)**
This is a high-speed "marathon." You have 120 minutes for 100 questions, meaning you have only **72 seconds per question**.

* **Duration:** 2 Hours (120 mins)
* **Total Marks:** 300
* **Total Questions:** 100 (All MCQs)
* **Marking Scheme:**
    * **Correct:** +3 marks
    * **Incorrect:** -1 mark
* **Crucial Note:** BARC often includes several "heavy" calculation questions to trap you. Success depends on skipping the 10-minute problems to find the 30-second conceptual ones.
